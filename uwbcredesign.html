UWBC Website Redesign

Sep – Nov 2017


University of Waterloo Badminton Club (UWBC) organizes and hosts sessions and events for its members. The club's website is the main source of information but it has been neglected for years.

Overview

Problem: The UWBC website is the main source of information available to the general public. The website has not been updated for decades and uses stock CSS from the university. Users' expectation and the nature of how they interact with websites have changed drastically.

Client: University of Waterloo Badminton Club – a non-profit, student-run club

Role: Project management and design

Note: This was an individual project which I took upon myself. As a former director of the club, I had a good understanding for the need of this project and strong connections within the club.

User Research

User Groups

  1. New/Prospective Members – never been in the club before but wishes to learn about 'how it works'

  2. Returning Members – familiar with club operations

  3. Managing Directors – responsible for maintaining and updating the club website but may not have web development skills

Original Design

Home Page

  • quick links accessible on the right
  • content in the middle looks condensed
  • there are many links which draw attention

To Join Page

  • includes perks and things to know during session
  • may be irrelevant for knowing how to join
  • lots of text

Level Information Link

  • link to PDF instead of being directly on a page
  • important enough to deserve a page of its own

Questionnaire

Created using Google Forms and consisted of:

  • Likert scale questions regarding appearance and ease of navigation
  • Likes
  • Dislikes
  • Open-ended suggestions

Findings

Likes

  • "I could immediately see what times the next sessions were"
  • "Simple to use"
  • "News feed is right on the homepage"
  • "Has a familiar feel"
  • "Important information can be found on the right"

Dislikes

  • "Looks cluttered"
  • "There are too many words"
  • "Not enough pictures"
  • "Very ugly and outdated"
  • "Too much going on to effectively determine where to focus attention"

User Goals

New/Prospective Members

  • create expectations for joining the club
  • learn everything they need to know before committing
  • be able to contact a person

Returning Members

  • be informed of any new changes/updates
  • have access to the new schedule of sessions/events

Managing Directors

  • able to make changes and updates on the website without investing much time or effort

Project Goals

  1. Update the website's aesthetics
  2. Simplify the website's navigation by having it intuitive and easy to learn
  3. Accommodate different users' goals and tasks

Wireframes & Mockups

Usability Testing

InVision was used to create a clickable prototype from the initial mockups. I wanted to evaluate how easy/difficult it was for users to find certain content on the website.

Users were provided a scenario relating to one of the popular user tasks. For example:

"You don't have any equipment and are unsure of what you're supposed to bring to session or what options you have. Where would you find more information about this?"

Protocol

  1. Present a brief outline of the test and permission to record
  2. Have them fill out general participant information (to categorize into a user group)
  3. Present the prototype
  4. Present scenarios with follow up questions
    • encourage them to think-aloud
  5. Concluding survey
    • rating appearance and ease of navigation; likes, dislikes, and recommendations
    • allow them to explore the website as normal

Findings

Metrics

Duration: time to complete the task

Number of Errors: number of times the user clicked to a different page or went in the opposite direction

Ease: on a scale from 1 (very difficult) to 5 (very easy), the user was asked to rate the task upon completion

Values are averaged from the number of participants who completed each task

Likes

  • "Clear menu"
  • "Easy to navigate and look for categories"
  • "The more important information stands out"
  • "Simple with open concept"

Dislikes

  • "Might be easier for those who have been in the club before"
  • "Harder to navigate for specific things like challenge rules"
  • "Logic of information placement was slightly not to my expectations"

Summary

Users perform and think differently. Given the same task, individuals may take different approaches or have different navigation mental models.

The tasks used target finding specific information but some users may consume information by browsing without needing to find anything in particular.

Overall improvement compared to the original website based on 'appearance' and 'ease of navigation' which scored 4.8 and 4.0 on a 5-point Likert scale respectively (this can be compared to the baseline which scored 2.4 and 3.3 respectively).

Final Design

I have decided to present just the Home page for the iterations and the Final InVision Prototype.

Aside from user feedback I received from testing, I also got some feedback form friends who work as product designers and web developers.

Although the initial prototype seemed to be an overall improvement from the original design, the usability test helped me understand what components still needed to addressed allowed me to move on with further iteration.

Feedback (Version 1)

  • colour palette of gold seemed too corporate
  • 'This Term' label needs to be more clear
  • Reorganize the primary nav and ordering in which new users should go through the website

Version 2

Feedback (Version 2)

  • use white text on images (not blue) for accessibilty
  • use a scrim for the navigation bar
  • make tints darker for greater contrast
  • rework 'How it Works' section since it is important for new members
  • have event cards just link to the Facebook page (easier for maintenance)
  • add more padding to the top and bottom of each section
  • CTA button labels should be clear to let users know exactly where it will lead to

Version 3

Reflection

Although I had a good idea of how each step in the design process would pan out, putting it into practise provided really good experience. The feedback from both users and experts tremendously helped me understand what to look for and how to design with intention and care.
