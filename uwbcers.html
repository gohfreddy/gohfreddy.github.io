UWBC Exec Recognition System

University of Waterloo Badminton Club (UWBC) depends on its execs to maintain operations and services for the general members. Without the execs, there would be no club.

Problem

Data shows:

  • execs serve on average 2.26 terms (1 school term = 4 months)
  • 45% of execs only served for 1 term
  • 14 execs returned in 2016 out of the 77 execs from 2015

This is a completely voluntary position but critical to club operations. And fewer execs were returning to the club. Thereâ€™s many possible reasons and some of them are out of our control.

Solution

I wanted to help make the exec experience as positive as possible.

Teams - Execs worked together to create their own team name and logo (sketch). I would vectorize and colour their logos in Sketch.

Points - Entry was done using a Google Form. We tracked the exec name, denomination of points, category, and a description of action.

Badges - Point categories helped execs understand how they can earn points for their team. Categories included: creativity, enthusiasm, event helper, mentoring, initiative. Exec socials turned into competitive events (like a relay race or game show) to earn special badges and points.

Rewards - Points were converted into rewards. Highest point earners from each team was treated to bubble tea and the winning team was rewarded with a free dinner.

My Role

This project was initiated out of self-interest. What started out as friendly competition to motivate execs, turned into investigating a serious underlying issue we had.

I was responsible for concept creation, graphic design, creating new team challenges, and managing logistics. All of the directors helped record scores, organize team events, and mentor their assigned execs. Thanks to Ahmed for helping maintain our static leaderboard throughout the terms.
